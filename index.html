About Me

I am a nuclear astrophysicist specializing in high-resolution spectroscopy, chemodynamics, and the study of stellar populations in the Milky Way. My work has led to breakthroughs in understanding the chemodynamical history of the Galactic halo, the role of r-process nucleosynthesis, and the evolution of globular clusters.

Research Interests

  • r-process nucleosynthesis and tracing progenitor supernovae
  • Assembly history and chemodynamical evolution of the Milky Way
  • Abundances and kinematics of the stellar populations in Galactic halo and globular clusters
  • High-resolution spectroscopy in optical, UV and NIR to understand complete nucleosynthesis history of stars

Future Research Plans

I plan to explore the chemical signatures of the early Universe using advanced spectroscopic data and model the contributions of neutron star mergers to the Galactic r-process. This includes utilizing cutting-edge spectroscopic surveys to deepen our understanding of the Galactic halo's formation and evolution. Currently I am working on understanding the stellar populations of ancient globular clusters in the MW halo using data from Gemini South and GTC.
