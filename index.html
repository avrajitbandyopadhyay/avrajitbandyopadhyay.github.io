About Me

I am a nuclear astrophysicist specializing in high-resolution spectroscopy, chemodynamics, and the study of stellar populations in the Milky Way. My work has led to breakthroughs in understanding the chemodynamical history of the Galactic halo, the role of r-process nucleosynthesis, and the evolution of globular clusters.

Research Interests

  • High-resolution spectroscopy and kinematics of halo stars and globular clusters
  • r-process nucleosynthesis and tracing progenitor supernovae
  • Assembly history and chemodynamical evolution of the Milky Way
  • Stellar populations to understand Galactic structure

Future Research Plans

I plan to explore the chemical signatures of the early Universe using advanced spectroscopic data and model the contributions of neutron star mergers to the Galactic r-process. This includes utilizing cutting-edge surveys like 4MOST and PFS to deepen our understanding of the Galactic halo's formation and evolution.
